It's been a little under two weeks since I've had my ACL reconstruction operation on my left knee and I was really nervous going to my check-up appointment. I had high hopes in them saying I could walk without my crutches; I hate those things. The only good thing that came out of it was me getting the ok to finally take off my bandages and being complemented on how well my progress in bending my knee is coming along. At the end of the visit, the doctor was impressed as to how fast I put my brace back on. I'm thinking That's interesting, don't all patients start to get pretty fast at this after some time.
Later on I was surprised with a burrito when my mother came home from work and it honestly made my day. I vaguely mentioned a craving of my favorite burrito in pasing the other day and here she is walking through the door with it; I love that woman. Towards the end of the night, I tried to complete a homework assignment (Lab 2) a week earlier but my lab partner and I got stuck, we spent three hours trouble shooting the error and got no where; that was rather frustrating but at least we got a "good" start. I called it quits for the day after that fiasco and just hit the sack. I'd say it was a pretty productive day though overall.

I had no idea when I was leaving to drive to LA. I did not want to miss any lectures for that day, but knew I was going to have to miss one so that I don't arrive too late. It was going to have to be my 4pm lecture. The one I was trying to avoid, of course. I hate watching zoom recordings of the class. My friend, who was carpooling with me, and I decided to try and get to LA at least by 8. We left around noon. There goes another lecture recording I have to watch later...
Being able to catch up with an old friend while driving always makes the trip go along faster. Even though it was a 6.5 hour drive, it definitely didn't feel like that. Forgot how much of a character my friend is, she definitely embodied much of what the Bay represents. As soon as I gave her control of the music, the first song she played I immediately recognized as it was blaring through the speakers. Cupcake No Fillin is a calssic Bay Area anthem!! It was a great car ride to say the least.
It had been so long since I was able to sit down at a restaurant, so with LA recently lifting that restriction I naturally had to take my girlfirend out to dinner. We decided on Cheesecake Factory and I couldn't have been happier. The wait of almost an hour was brutal but all I could keep thinking about was that chocolate bread and the appetizers I was about to order. Went to bed with a food baby, and I wasn't mad.
